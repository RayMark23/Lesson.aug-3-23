Philippine Constitution

The current Constitution of the Philippines was ratified on February 2, 1987. It replaced the previous 1973 Constitution and has since served as the fundamental law of the land. The Constitution outlines the structure of the government, defines the rights and responsibilities of citizens, and establishes the framework for the rule of law and the country's democratic system.

Here are some key features and provisions of the 1987 Philippine Constitution:

  1. Preamble:The Constitution begins with a preamble that sets out the aspirations and guiding principles of the Filipino people, including their commitment to democracy, social justice, and equality.

  2. Article I - National Territory:This article defines the national territory of the Philippines, which includes the archipelago, its territorial sea, and other maritime zones.

  3. Article II - Declaration of Principles and State Policies:This article lays down the basic principles and policies that guide the government and promote the welfare of the people. It includes provisions on social justice, human rights, and the pursuit of an independent foreign policy.

  4. Article III - Bill of Rights:One of the most crucial parts of the Constitution, this article enshrines the fundamental rights and liberties of Filipino citizens, such as the right to life, liberty, due process, and equal protection under the law.

  5. Article IV - Citizenship:This article sets the criteria for Philippine citizenship and outlines the processes for acquiring and losing citizenship.

  6. Article V - Suffrage:This article defines the qualifications and procedures for voter registration and the conduct of elections in the country.

  7. Article VI - The Legislative Department:This article establishes the Congress of the Philippines as a bicameral legislature, consisting of the Senate and the House of Representatives, and defines their powers and responsibilities.

  8. Article VII - The Executive Department:This article outlines the powers and functions of the President, including the qualifications for running for the position and the term of office.

  9. Article VIII - The Judicial Department:This article establishes the independence of the judiciary and outlines the powers of the Supreme Court and other lower courts.

  10. Article IX - Constitutional Commissions:This article establishes the Constitutional Commissions, including the Commission on Elections (COMELEC), the Civil Service Commission (CSC), and the Commission on Audit (COA).

  11. Article X - Local Government:This article provides for the creation and organization of local government units, their powers, and the relationship between the national government and local government.

  12. Article XI - Accountability of Public Officers:This article deals with the accountability of public officials and the establishment of the Office of the Ombudsman to investigate and prosecute cases of corruption.

  13. Article XII - National Economy and Patrimony:This article contains provisions on the national economy, promoting economic nationalism and protecting natural resources and industries.

  14. Article XIII - Social Justice and Human Rights:This article emphasizes the promotion of social justice and human rights, particularly for marginalized sectors of society.

  15. Article XIV - Education, Science, and Technology, Arts, Culture, and Sports:This article outlines the state's policies and responsibilities in the field of education and culture.

  16. Article XV - The Family:This article highlights the role of the family as a basic social institution and defines marriage and family relations.

  17. Article XVI - General Provisions:This article includes miscellaneous provisions, including amendments to the Constitution and transitory provisions.
